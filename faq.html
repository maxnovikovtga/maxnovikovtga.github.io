What is Sixley and how does it work?

Sixley is a new way to hire great people through personal recommendations. You can find out more about Sixley here.

How much money will I get if I recommend someone?

Sixley charges companies a fee to hire people. We share some of that money with the people who worked together to find successful candidates. Say there’s £1000 available for people who help. If you’re sent the job by the hirer and find the successful candidate immediately, you’ll receive the £1000. If you suggest someone else who knows the right person, and they recommend the person who is hired, then you’ll share the reward and get £500 each.

What does ‘up to’ mean when you talk about rewards?

We explain above how rewards get split between people. We don’t know at the outset how many people will need to work together to find a successful candidate for a particular role. So, we can only speak about the total reward that will be split between everyone who helps.

For hirers

Why the name Sixley?

We believe that every hirer is connected to the right hire through six degrees of talent separation. Hiring companies just don’t know who that perfect candidate is or how they’re connected to them.

Sixley was created to join the dots through facilitating, incentivising and managing personal recommendations.

How is Sixley different from other employment-related platforms such as LinkedIn and Indeed?

Indeed is a jobs board, LinkedIn is a CV bank - both are (largely) passive and lead to hundreds or thousands of unqualified and unfiltered applications that need huge amounts of work to sort through.

Neither platform presents personally-recommended candidates – recommendations on LinkedIn are generic and aren’t about how suitable a candidate would be for a specific role or company.

Sixley creates a shortlist of candidates that have been recommended and are interested in a specific role – not via an algorithm or AI but through personal, human recommendations from a peer in the industry.
